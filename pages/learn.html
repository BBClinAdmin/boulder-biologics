Is stem cell therapy FDA-approved?

No — with one important exception. The FDA has approved certain hematopoietic stem cell transplants for specific blood and immune disorders, but no stem cell or cellular therapy is approved for the conditions most patients are searching about.

About these explainers

These pages exist because most patient-facing material on stem cell therapy is either overpromised marketing or impenetrable technical writing. We write what we say to patients at consultation. Where we use a regulatory passage verbatim from the FDA or from our own clinical disclosures, we mark it clearly. Where the evidence is preliminary, we say so. Where a claim doesn't survive peer review, we don't make it.

Important regulatory information

Autologous bone marrow– and adipose-derived cellular therapy is not approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) as a drug or biologic for the treatment of specific musculoskeletal or systemic conditions. The approaches described are experimental, grounded in evolving translational and early clinical research, and offered only following comprehensive evaluation and informed consent. At Boulder Biologics, we use autologous biologic material only — no donor-derived, umbilical cord, placental, or amniotic products. See our FDA Guidelines page for full regulatory context.
