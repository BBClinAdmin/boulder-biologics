What you can offer your patients

Four treatments. Your choice of any or all.

A small set of point-of-care procedures, each using the patient's own biology. A practice can offer any combination — the mix is always yours.

PRP

Platelet-rich plasma

The patient's own platelets, concentrated in a centrifuge and injected to support healing in tendons, ligaments, and joints.

Prolo

Prolotherapy

Targeted injections that prompt the body's own repair response in worn or lax ligaments and tendons.

BMAC

Bone marrow aspirate concentrate

Cells drawn from the patient's own marrow and concentrated, then placed where more support is needed.

mFAT

Microfragmented adipose

The patient's own fat tissue, micro-fragmented and rinsed within the same procedure and delivered as autologous structural support.

Why building it alone is hard

Your patients want this. Building it right is the hard part.

Autologous biologics is one of the most attractive cash-pay service lines in musculoskeletal care. But most practices have no clear, reliable path — the barriers are real, and hard to clear piece by piece.

01

The right equipment

Centrifuges, kits, and lab instrumentation take specialized knowledge to spec and size correctly.

02

A protocol library

Building clinical SOPs, consent, and safety frameworks from scratch is time-intensive and easy to get wrong.

03

A compliant space

Designing a sterile procedure room and lab to environmental standards requires real, hands-on experience.

04

Physician technique

Learning to perform these procedures takes the case volume and mentorship a single practice can't self-generate.

We partner with you to walk through all of it — start to finish.
